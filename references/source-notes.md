# Source Notes

## PubMed

- Best for indexed biomedical literature.
- Strong choice when you need stable metadata and abstracts.
- A PubMed hit is not automatically open access.

## Europe PMC

- Good for biomedical literature plus preprints.
- Often includes abstracts and Europe PMC links for preprint servers such as bioRxiv and medRxiv.
- Use this as the main preprint discovery source when possible.

## Google Scholar

- Broad discovery layer.
- Good for finding adjacent literature, citations, and cross-domain work.
- Metadata quality is less consistent than PubMed.
- Treat Scholar snippets as discovery evidence, not final evidence, unless confirmed elsewhere.

## arXiv

- Useful for quantitative, computational, imaging, signal processing, and ML-heavy methods.
- Less central than PubMed or Europe PMC for wet-lab biomedical papers.

## Ranking rule

For biomedical literature discovery:

1. PubMed / Europe PMC abstract-backed hits
2. Preprints with clear abstracts and stable links
3. Google Scholar-only hits without strong metadata
