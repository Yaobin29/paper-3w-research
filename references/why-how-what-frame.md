# WHY HOW WHAT Frame

Use this rubric to convert search hits, abstracts, or paper pages into a decision-friendly summary.

## WHY

Answer the motivation layer, not just the topic label.

Look for:

- the failure mode or bottleneck in current methods,
- the unmet biological, clinical, engineering, or analytical need,
- the explicit research question or hypothesis.

Preferred wording:

- `This work targets ... because ...`
- `The key bottleneck is ...`
- `The paper asks whether ...`

## HOW

Answer the mechanism or implementation layer.

Look for:

- model system,
- material / device / assay / algorithm,
- technical innovation point,
- what is different from prior work.

Preferred wording:

- `The authors use ... to ...`
- `The main innovation is ...`
- `Compared with prior work, this changes ...`

## WHAT

Answer the evidence and implication layer.

Look for:

- main result, output, or artifact,
- what the data supports,
- limitation, remaining uncertainty, or next-step gap.

Preferred wording:

- `They found / built / showed ...`
- `This suggests ...`
- `A remaining gap is ...`

## Biomedical emphasis

When the topic is biomedical engineering or translational research, prefer these lenses:

- model relevance,
- sample size or validation depth,
- clinical or biological significance,
- manufacturability / scalability,
- reproducibility,
- missing controls,
- comparison against current standard.

## Do not overclaim

- If you only have title + abstract, say `based on the abstract`.
- If the result claim is weak, say `suggests` rather than `proves`.
- If the paper is a preprint, explicitly mark `preprint, not yet peer reviewed`.
